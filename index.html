📖 The Story Behind TPU

How a voice search problem led to a revolution in AI hardware

🔊 The Problem (2013)

Google's Android Voice Search was growing rapidly. Jeff Dean and Jonathan Ross calculated:

"If every Android user used voice search for just 3 minutes per day, Google would need to double its entire data center capacity."

CPUs and GPUs were simply too inefficient and power-hungry for this scale.

💡 The Insight

Neural networks are essentially massive matrix multiplications. CPUs waste 80% of their energy on:

  • Instruction decoding
  • Branch prediction
  • Cache management
  • Control logic

What if we built a chip that ONLY does matrix math?

⚡ The Solution

Google's small team designed TPU v1 in just 15 months—unprecedented speed for custom silicon.

Deployed in 2016, it powered Google Search, Translate, and AlphaGo.

Result: 10-30x better energy efficiency than GPUs for AI workloads.

⚡ What is an ASIC & TPU?

Application-Specific Integrated Circuit - Built for ONE purpose

🔪

Swiss Army Knife (CPU)

Can cut, screw, open bottles, saw wood... but each tool is mediocre.

Great for everyday tasks, but not specialized.

⚔️

Ninja Sword (ASIC/TPU)

Does ONE thing perfectly: cuts with extreme precision and speed.

Can't open a bottle, but slices better than anything else.

🔧 What is ASIC?

Application-Specific Integrated Circuit

Custom silicon designed for exactly one task. Examples:

  • Bitcoin mining chips (mine only Bitcoin)
  • Google TPU (only does AI matrix math)
  • Apple Neural Engine (only does on-device AI)

Trade-off: Maximum efficiency, zero flexibility.

🧠 What is TPU?

Tensor Processing Unit

Google's ASIC specifically designed for tensor operations (the math behind AI).

Key innovation: Systolic Array

  • No instruction decoding
  • No cache management
  • Data flows like a heartbeat
  • 10-30x more efficient than GPU

📚 Key Concepts in TPU Architecture

📐 What is a Tensor?

A multi-dimensional array - the fundamental data structure in AI:

  • 0D: Scalar (single number) - e.g., temperature = 25°C
  • 1D: Vector (list) - e.g., [1, 2, 3, 4, 5]
  • 2D: Matrix (grid) - e.g., image pixels
  • 3D+: Tensor (cube/hypercube) - e.g., video frames, model weights

AI models process massive tensors (billions of numbers). TPU is optimized for these operations.

🚧 Von Neumann Bottleneck

The fundamental limit of traditional computers:

CPU must constantly fetch data from memory → process → store back. This back-and-forth is slower than processing itself!

Analogy: A chef who must walk to a distant pantry for EVERY ingredient instead of having them on the counter.

TPU Solution: Systolic array keeps data flowing internally - no pantry trips needed!

🔢 Quantization

Reducing precision to save memory and speed up computation:

Format Bits Example
FP32 32 bits 3.14159265
FP16 16 bits 3.14
INT8 8 bits 3
INT4 4 bits 3 (approx)

Trade-off: Less precision → faster & smaller, but slightly less accurate. Modern AI uses INT8/INT4 for inference!

🧊 bfloat16 (Brain Floating Point)

Google's custom number format optimized for AI:

  • Same range as FP32 (can represent big/small numbers)
  • Same size as FP16 (16 bits, half the memory)
  • Best of both worlds!

Used in TPU v2+ for training. Allows faster computation without losing the "dynamic range" needed for neural networks.

🖥️ CISC vs TPU

CISC = Complex Instruction Set Computer (like Intel x86)

  • Many complex instructions
  • Variable instruction length
  • Heavy decoding overhead

TPU is NOT CISC! TPU uses minimal instructions - essentially just "multiply and add." This simplicity is why it's so efficient.

🧊 Liquid Cooling

Advanced cooling for high-density chips:

  • TPU v3+ generates massive heat (200W+ per chip)
  • Air cooling is insufficient at this density
  • Liquid cooling uses water/coolant flowing through cold plates
  • Allows chips to run at full power without overheating

Essential for data center TPUs running 24/7 at maximum performance.

🔧 TPU v1 Architecture Deep Dive

The original design that started the revolution (2016)

🧮 MMU - Matrix Multiply Unit

The heart of TPU v1:

  • 65,536 8-bit MAC units
  • Arranged in a 256×256 systolic array
  • Peak performance: 92 TFLOPS INT8
  • Consumes only 40W (vs 250W+ for GPU)

Each MAC unit does: Multiply → Add → Pass to neighbor

💾 24MB Unified Buffer

On-chip SRAM for activations:

  • Acts as a fast scratchpad for intermediate results
  • Much faster than external HBM memory
  • Reduces trips to main memory
  • Critical for keeping the systolic array fed with data

Analogy: Like a chef's cutting board (fast access) vs. the pantry (slow access).

📦 Other Components

  • Weight FIFO: Buffers model weights from HBM
  • Activation Unit: Applies non-linear functions (ReLU, Sigmoid)
  • HBM: High-bandwidth memory for storing the full model
  • PCIe: Connects TPU to host server

Small team starts TPU v1 design. Goal: 10x efficiency improvement over GPU for inference.

First deployment! 92 TFLOPS INT8, 40W. Powers Google Search, Translate, and AlphaGo vs. Lee Sedol.

2017

🎓 TPU v2

Adds training capability (not just inference). 180 TFLOPS. Enables larger model training.

2018

2x performance, introduces liquid cooling. First "pod" scale - 1,024 chips working together.

🌐 TPU v4

3D torus interconnect (ICI). 4,096 chips per pod. Powers large language models.

2023

⚡ TPU v5p

Scales to 8,960 chips per pod. Trains Gemini 1.0. Massive scale-out capability.

2024

🧠 TPU v6e (Trillium)

Introduces SparseCore for embeddings and Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) models.

2025

🏆 TPU v7 (Ironwood)

Inference-first design. 192 GB HBM3E, 42.5 FP4 exaflops per pod. Massive memory boost.

2026

🔀 TPU v8 (8t & 8i)

First split design! 8t for training, 8i for inference. 384 MB SRAM, Boardfly topology.

🌐 OCS & TPU Pods

Scaling beyond a single chip

🔄 OCS - Optical Circuit Switching

Technology that connects thousands of TPUs with light instead of copper wires:

  • Uses fiber optics for chip-to-chip communication
  • Reconfigurable: can change topology on the fly
  • Much faster and lower power than electrical switching
  • Enables the 3D torus and Boardfly topologies

📦 TPU Pods

Clusters of TPUs working as one supercomputer:

  • TPU v3 Pod: 1,024 chips
  • TPU v4 Pod: 4,096 chips
  • TPU v5p Pod: 8,960 chips
  • TPU v8 Pod: Up to 9,600 chips (theoretical millions)

All chips share memory and work on the same model simultaneously!

🔄 TensorFlow & TPU

The software that brings TPU to life

📦 What is TensorFlow?

Google's open-source machine learning framework:

  • Represents models as computational graphs
  • Operations = nodes, Tensors = edges
  • Automatically optimizes for hardware
  • Works on CPU, GPU, and TPU

TensorFlow was literally created to program TPUs!

⚡ XLA Compiler

Accelerated Linear Algebra:

  • Compiles TensorFlow graphs to TPU machine code
  • Optimizes memory layout for systolic arrays
  • Fuses operations to reduce overhead
  • Also works for GPU optimization

🔀 TPU v8: The Revolutionary Split

Why one chip is no longer enough

Key Insight: Training and Inference have become so different that a single chip design is a compromise. Google split TPU v8 into two specialized chips for the first time!

🔥 TPU 8t

Training Chip

Focus: Maximum throughput

Bottleneck: Compute-bound

Scale: Up to millions of chips

Key Feature: Massive gradient sharing

Memory: Optimized for model weights

Power: Higher (training is intensive)

Analogy: A factory that builds cars - needs maximum production capacity

❄️ TPU 8i

Inference Chip

Focus: Minimum latency

Bottleneck: Memory-bound

Scale: Thousands of chips

Key Feature: 384 MB on-chip SRAM

Memory: KV cache optimization

Power: Lower (efficiency critical)

Analogy: A race car - needs instant response, not just speed

Why the Split? The KV Cache Problem

Training (8t)

Process ALL tokens at once

📚 → 🧠

Forward + Backward pass

Needs: Compute power

Inference (8i)

Generate ONE token at a time

💬 → ⏱️ → 💬

Must access KV cache every token

Needs: Fast memory access

384 MB SRAM in 8i stores the KV cache on-chip, eliminating slow HBM access!

🆕 New in TPU v8

  • Boardfly Topology: Hierarchical interconnect replacing 3D torus
  • CAE: Collectives Acceleration Engine for distributed operations
  • Axion CPU: Google's ARM-based host processor
  • 2nm Process: TSMC's most advanced manufacturing
  • 288 GB HBM: Massive memory per chip

💡 Interesting Facts About TPU

⚡ Speed of Development

TPU v1 was designed and deployed in just 15 months—a record for custom silicon. Most chips take 3-5 years!

🌍 Energy Impact

TPU v1 used 40W vs GPU's 250W+ for similar AI tasks. At Google's scale, this saves enough energy to power a small city!

🏆 AlphaGo Moment

When AlphaGo beat Lee Sedol, it used 48 TPUs. The match was watched by 280 million people—TPU's first global spotlight!

💰 The "NVIDIA Tax"

NVIDIA's profit margins are ~70%. Google builds TPUs to avoid paying this premium—saving billions annually at scale.

🔒 Cloud Only

You cannot buy a TPU! They are only available through Google Cloud. Google keeps them exclusive to maintain competitive advantage.

🧠 Gemini's Scale

Training Gemini Ultra required tens of thousands of TPU v4/v5p chips running for months. The electricity bill was in the millions!

📱 In Your Pocket

Google's Pixel phones use smaller versions of TPU technology (Edge TPU) for on-device AI like photo enhancement and voice recognition.

🌡️ Liquid Cooling

TPU v3+ data centers use enough liquid coolant to fill multiple Olympic swimming pools. The heat is recycled to warm nearby buildings!

🎮 Free Access

Google Colab offers free TPU access (v2/v3) to students and researchers worldwide. Your presentation could run on a TPU!

🎯 Conclusion

📌 Key Takeaways

  • TPU is Google's custom ASIC for AI, born from a voice search scaling crisis
  • Systolic arrays eliminate the Von Neumann bottleneck
  • From v1 (2016) to v8 (2026): 110x performance improvement
  • v8 split (8t/8i) represents a paradigm shift in AI hardware
  • TPU powers Gemini, Search, AlphaGo, and more
  • The future is specialization, not generalization

🔮 The Future

  • More specialized chips (training vs inference vs edge)
  • Higher quantization (INT4, INT2, binary neural networks)
  • Optical computing for even faster interconnects
  • Neuromorphic chips (brain-like computing)
  • TPU v9, v10... the evolution continues!
